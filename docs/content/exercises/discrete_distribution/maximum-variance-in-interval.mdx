## Exercise

Let $\Gamma$ be the set of random variables $X$ such that $X \in [-1,1]$ almost surely.

Determine:

$$
X^* \in \arg\max_{X \in \Gamma} \operatorname{Var}(X)
$$

## Hint

Use the formula $\operatorname{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - (\mathbb{E}[X])^2$. What is the maximum possible value of $\mathbb{E}[X^2]$ given the constraint?

## Hint

To maximize variance, you need $\mathbb{E}[X^2] = 1$ and $\mathbb{E}[X] = 0$. What does the first condition tell you about the support of $X$?

## Solution

For any random variable $X \in [-1,1]$ almost surely, we have:

$$
\operatorname{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - \big(\mathbb{E}[X]\big)^2
$$

Since $X^2 \leq 1$, we have $\mathbb{E}[X^2] \leq 1$. Thus:

$$
\operatorname{Var}(X) \leq 1 - \big(\mathbb{E}[X]\big)^2 \leq 1
$$

To achieve equality $\operatorname{Var}(X) = 1$, we need:

$$
\mathbb{E}[X^2] = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad \mathbb{E}[X] = 0
$$

The condition $\mathbb{E}[X^2] = 1$ with $X^2 \leq 1$ forces $|X| = 1$ almost surely, so $X \in \{-1, 1\}$ almost surely.

Next, the condition $\mathbb{E}[X] = 0$ implies:

$$
\mathbb{P}(X = 1) = \mathbb{P}(X = -1) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

Thus, a maximizer is the symmetric **Rademacher variable**:

$$
X^* = \begin{cases}
1, & \text{with probability } \frac{1}{2}, \\[6pt]
-1, & \text{with probability } \frac{1}{2}
\end{cases}
\qquad \text{and} \qquad \boxed{\operatorname{Var}(X^*) = 1}
$$

**Remark.** More generally, if $X \in [a,b]$ almost surely, we have Popoviciu's inequality:

$$
\operatorname{Var}(X) \leq \frac{(b-a)^2}{4}
$$

with equality when $\mathbb{P}(X = a) = \mathbb{P}(X = b) = \frac{1}{2}$.
