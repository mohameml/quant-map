## Exercise

At a bus stop, two bus lines serve the stop:

- Bus 1 passes every 2 minutes,
- Bus 2 passes every 5 minutes.

A traveler arrives at a completely random time (independent of the schedules).

What is the average wait time before the next bus (regardless of which line)?

## Hint

Model the wait times for each bus as uniform random variables. Use the formula $\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)] = \int_0^\infty \mathbb{P}(\min(T_1, T_2) > t) \, dt$.

## Hint

Since $T_1$ and $T_2$ are independent, $\mathbb{P}(\min(T_1, T_2) > t) = \mathbb{P}(T_1 > t) \cdot \mathbb{P}(T_2 > t)$.

## Solution

The passages of the two buses form a process where:

$$
T_1 \sim \mathcal{U}[0,2], \qquad T_2 \sim \mathcal{U}[0,5]
$$

represent the respective wait times if we only considered one line.

We seek:

$$
\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)]
$$

**Recall: Expectation of a positive random variable**

For $X \geq 0$ continuous:

$$
\mathbb{E}[X] = \int_0^\infty \mathbb{P}(X > t) \, dt
$$

Since $\min(T_1, T_2)$ is a positive random variable and $T_1$ and $T_2$ are independent:

$$
\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)] = \int_0^\infty \mathbb{P}(\min(T_1, T_2) > t) \, dt = \int_0^\infty \mathbb{P}(T_1 > t) \cdot \mathbb{P}(T_2 > t) \, dt
$$

For $X \sim \mathcal{U}[a,b]$:

$$
F_X(x) = \begin{cases}
0, & x < a \\[6pt]
\dfrac{x-a}{b-a}, & a \leq x \leq b \\[10pt]
1, & x > b
\end{cases}
$$

Therefore:

$$
\mathbb{P}(T_1 > t) = \begin{cases}
1 - \dfrac{t}{2}, & 0 \leq t \leq 2 \\
0, & t > 2
\end{cases}
\qquad
\mathbb{P}(T_2 > t) = \begin{cases}
1 - \dfrac{t}{5}, & 0 \leq t \leq 5 \\
0, & t > 5
\end{cases}
$$

Since $2 < 5$, the integral reduces to $[0,2]$:

$$
\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)] = \int_0^2 \left(1 - \frac{t}{2}\right)\left(1 - \frac{t}{5}\right) dt
$$

Expanding:

$$
\left(1 - \frac{t}{2}\right)\left(1 - \frac{t}{5}\right) = 1 - \frac{7}{10}t + \frac{t^2}{10}
$$

Therefore:

$$
\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)] = \left[ t - \frac{7}{20}t^2 + \frac{1}{30}t^3 \right]_0^2 = 2 - \frac{7}{20} \cdot 4 + \frac{1}{30} \cdot 8 = 2 - \frac{28}{20} + \frac{8}{30}
$$

Thus:

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[\min(T_1, T_2)] = \frac{52}{60} = \frac{13}{15}}
$$

approximately 52 seconds.
