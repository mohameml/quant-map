## Exercise

Let $X_1, X_2, \dots$ be a sequence of independent and identically distributed random variables, each following the uniform distribution $\mathcal{U}[0,1]$.

Define:

$$
N := \min \left\{ n \geq 1 \mid \sum_{i=1}^n X_i > 1 \right\}
$$

In other words, $N$ is the number of draws needed for the sum to exceed 1.

**Question:** Determine the expectation $\mathbb{E}[N]$.

## Hint

Use the formula $\mathbb{E}[N] = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \mathbb{P}(N > n)$. What does the event $\{N > n\}$ mean in terms of the partial sum?

## Hint

Calculate $\mathbb{P}(X_1 + \cdots + X_n \leq 1)$ by recognizing this as the volume of a simplex in $[0,1]^n$.

## Hint

The volume of the simplex $\{(x_1, \dots, x_n) \in [0,1]^n \mid x_1 + \cdots + x_n \leq 1\}$ is $\frac{1}{n!}$. Sum this over all $n \geq 0$.

## Solution

We use the general expectation formula for a positive integer-valued random variable:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N] = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \mathbb{P}(N \geq n) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \mathbb{P}(N > n)
$$

By definition of $N$, we have:

$$
\mathbb{P}(N > n) = \mathbb{P} \left( \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \leq 1 \right)
$$

Let:

$$
P_n := \mathbb{P} \left( \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \leq 1 \right)
$$

Then:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N] = \sum_{n=0}^\infty P_n
$$

**Calculating $P_n$**

We seek:

$$
P_n = \mathbb{P}\left( X_1 + \cdots + X_n \leq 1 \right) = \int_{[0,1]^n} \mathbf{1}_{\{x_1 + \cdots + x_n \leq 1\}} \, dx_1 \cdots dx_n
$$

This domain is the **simplex**:

$$
\Delta_n := \left\{ (x_1, \dots, x_n) \in [0,1]^n \mid \sum x_i \leq 1 \right\}
$$

This simplex has a known volume:

$$
\text{Vol}(\Delta_n) = \frac{1}{n!}
$$

_(This classical result can be proven by induction or by integrating successively over $x_1, \dots, x_n$)_

**Conclusion**

We therefore have:

$$
\mathbb{E}[N] = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{n!} = e
$$

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[N] = e}
$$

**Geometric illustration for $n = 2$**

For $n = 2$, we consider:

$$
P_2 = \mathbb{P}(X_1 + X_2 \leq 1) = \int_{[0,1]^2} \mathbf{1}_{\{x + y \leq 1\}} \, dx \, dy
$$

The integration domain is a triangle with vertices $(0,0)$, $(1,0)$, $(0,1)$, forming a 2-dimensional simplex.

The area of this triangle is:

$$
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{2!}
$$

This confirms that:

$$
P_2 = \frac{1}{2!} \quad \text{and more generally} \quad P_n = \frac{1}{n!}
$$
