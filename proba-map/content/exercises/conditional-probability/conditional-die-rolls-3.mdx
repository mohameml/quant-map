## Problem

Dan rolls a fair six-sided die until he gets a $6$.
Given that he only observed even numbers throughout the entire experiment,
what is the expected number of rolls?

## Hints

<Hint n={1}>
	Define the event $E$ = "all observed outcomes (including the last roll) are
	even." On $E$, every roll before the final one must belong to $\{2, 4\}$,
	and the last roll is $6$. Use independence to compute $\mathbb{P}(N = n,\; E)$.
</Hint>

<Hint n={2}>
	Compute $\mathbb{P}(E)$ by summing $\mathbb{P}(N = n,\; E)$ over all
	$n \ge 1$ (geometric series), then find the conditional distribution
	$\mathbb{P}(N = n \mid E)$ and identify which well-known distribution it
	follows.
</Hint>

## Solution

We model the rolls as a sequence $(X_n)_{n\ge 1}$ of i.i.d. random variables uniform on
$\{1,2,3,4,5,6\}$, i.e.

$$
\mathbb{P}(X_n=k)=\frac{1}{6},\qquad k\in\{1,2,3,4,5,6\}.
$$

Let

$$
N=\inf\{n\ge 1:\; X_n=6\}
$$

be the total number of rolls until the first $6$.

We condition on the event

$$
E=\{\text{all observed outcomes (up to and including the stopping time) are even}\}.
$$

On $E$, every roll before stopping must belong to $\{2,4\}$, and the last roll equals $6$.
Thus, for all $n\ge 1$,

$$
\{N=n\}\cap E=\{X_1\in\{2,4\},\dots,X_{n-1}\in\{2,4\},\,X_n=6\}.
$$

By independence,

$$
\mathbb{P}(N=n,\;E)=\left(\frac{2}{6}\right)^{n-1}\frac{1}{6}
=\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}\frac{1}{6}.
$$

**Computing $\mathbb{P}(E)$.**
We sum over $n\ge 1$:

$$
\mathbb{P}(E)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\mathbb{P}(N=n,\;E)
=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}\frac{1}{6}
=\frac{1}{6}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}.
$$

Now

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}
=\frac{1}{1-\frac{1}{3}}=\frac{3}{2},
$$

so

$$
\mathbb{P}(E)=\frac{1}{6}\cdot\frac{3}{2}=\frac{1}{4}.
$$

**Conditional distribution of $N$ given $E$.**
For $n\ge 1$,

$$
\mathbb{P}(N=n\mid E)
=\frac{\mathbb{P}(N=n,\;E)}{\mathbb{P}(E)}
=\frac{\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}\frac{1}{6}}{\frac{1}{4}}
= \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^{n-1}\frac{2}{3}.
$$

Therefore

$$
N \mid E \sim \mathcal{G}\!\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)
$$

$$
\boxed{\mathbb{E}[N\mid \text{only even numbers}] = \frac{3}{2}.}
$$
