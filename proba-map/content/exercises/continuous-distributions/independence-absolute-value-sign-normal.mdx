## Exercise

Let $X \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$. We define:

$$
\mathrm{sign}(X) = \begin{cases}
+1 & \text{if } X \geq 0 \\
-1 & \text{if } X < 0
\end{cases}
$$

**Question:** Show that the random variables $|X|$ and $\mathrm{sign}(X)$ are independent.

## Hint

To prove independence, show that for all $y > 0$ and $s \in \{-1, 1\}$: $\mathbb{P}(|X| \leq y, \mathrm{sign}(X) = s) = \mathbb{P}(|X| \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(\mathrm{sign}(X) = s)$.

## Hint

Use the symmetry of the standard normal distribution: $\Phi(-y) = 1 - \Phi(y)$ and $\Phi(0) = \frac{1}{2}$.

## Solution

Let $X \sim \mathcal{N}(0,1)$. We define:

$$
Y := |X|, \quad S := \mathrm{sign}(X) = \begin{cases}
+1 & \text{if } X \geq 0 \\
-1 & \text{if } X < 0
\end{cases}
$$

**Objective:** Show that for all $y > 0$ and $s \in \{-1, 1\}$:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = s) = \mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = s)
$$

**Step 1: Calculate $\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = 1)$**

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = 1) = \mathbb{P}(0 \leq X \leq y) = \Phi(y) - \Phi(0)
$$

Since $\Phi(0) = \frac{1}{2}$:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = 1) = \Phi(y) - \frac{1}{2}
$$

**Step 2: Calculate $\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = 1)$**

**Calculate $\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y)$:**

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) = \mathbb{P}(-y \leq X \leq y) = \Phi(y) - \Phi(-y)
$$

Using $\Phi(-y) + \Phi(y) = 1$:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) = 2\Phi(y) - 1
$$

**Calculate $\mathbb{P}(S = 1)$:**

$$
\mathbb{P}(S = 1) = \mathbb{P}(X \geq 0) = 1 - \Phi(0) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

Therefore:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = 1) = (2\Phi(y) - 1) \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \Phi(y) - \frac{1}{2}
$$

Thus:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = 1) = \mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = 1)
$$

**Similarly, for $S = -1$:**

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = -1) = \mathbb{P}(-y \leq X < 0) = \Phi(0) - \Phi(-y)
$$

Using $\Phi(-y) = 1 - \Phi(y)$:

$$
\Phi(0) - \Phi(-y) = \frac{1}{2} - (1 - \Phi(y)) = \Phi(y) - \frac{1}{2}
$$

And:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = -1) = (2\Phi(y) - 1) \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \Phi(y) - \frac{1}{2}
$$

Therefore:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = -1) = \mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = -1)
$$

**Conclusion**

For all $y > 0$ and $s \in \{-1, +1\}$:

$$
\mathbb{P}(Y \leq y, S = s) = \mathbb{P}(Y \leq y) \cdot \mathbb{P}(S = s) \Rightarrow Y \perp S
$$

$$
\boxed{|X| \text{ and } \mathrm{sign}(X) \text{ are independent}}
$$
