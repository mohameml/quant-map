## Problem

A point is chosen uniformly at random on the unit circle. Let $X$ be its x-coordinate (abscissa). Compute $\text{Var}(X)$.

## Hints

<Hint n={1}>
Parameterize the random point by an angle $\Theta \sim \mathcal{U}([0, 2\pi])$, so that $X = \cos(\Theta)$. Then compute $\mathbb{E}[X]$ and $\mathbb{E}[X^2]$ using the identity $\cos^2(\theta) = \frac{1 + \cos(2\theta)}{2}$.
</Hint>

## Solution

**Parameterization:**

A point chosen uniformly on the unit circle can be parameterized by an angle $\Theta \sim \mathcal{U}([0, 2\pi])$. The coordinates of the point are:

$$
(X, Y) = (\cos(\Theta), \sin(\Theta))
$$

**Computing $\mathbb{E}[X]$:**

$$
\mathbb{E}[X] = \mathbb{E}[\cos(\Theta)]
= \int_0^{2\pi} \cos(\theta) \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi} \, d\theta
= \frac{1}{2\pi} \left[ \sin(\theta) \right]_0^{2\pi}
= \frac{1}{2\pi} \left( \sin(2\pi) - \sin(0) \right)
= 0
$$

**Computing $\mathbb{E}[X^2]$:**

$$
\mathbb{E}[X^2] = \mathbb{E}[\cos^2(\Theta)]
= \int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2(\theta) \cdot \frac{1}{2\pi} \, d\theta
$$

Using the trigonometric identity $\cos^2(\theta) = \frac{1 + \cos(2\theta)}{2}$:

$$
\mathbb{E}[X^2] = \frac{1}{4\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} \left( 1 + \cos(2\theta) \right) \, d\theta
= \frac{1}{4\pi} \left[ \theta + \frac{\sin(2\theta)}{2} \right]_0^{2\pi}
= \frac{1}{4\pi} \cdot 2\pi
= \frac{1}{2}
$$

**Computing the variance:**

$$
\text{Var}(X) = \mathbb{E}[X^2] - (\mathbb{E}[X])^2 = \frac{1}{2} - 0^2 = \boxed{\frac{1}{2}}
$$

**Remark:** By symmetry of the circle, we also have $\text{Var}(Y) = \frac{1}{2}$ for the y-coordinate.
